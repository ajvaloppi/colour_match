RGB is the colour model used by things like computers. It is an additive colour model, which means that all the colours mixed together makes white.

CMYK is the colour model used by things like paint and printers. It is a subtractive colour model, which means that al the colours mixed together makes black.

To get the best results, you will have to adjust the brightness and contrast of your screen.
In the image below, adjust your contrast until you can just see the difference of the last two white stripes (20 and 21).
Then, adjust your brightness until you can just see the difference of the first two black stripes (01 and 02).
