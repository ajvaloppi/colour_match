RGB is an additive colour model. Computer monitors and TVs use red, green, and blue light to display colours. Red and green light create yellow light, blue and green light create cyan light, red and blue light create magenta light, and all three make white light.

CMYK is a subtractive colour model. Printers and paints use cyan, magenta, yellow, and black pigments to display colours. Cyan and magenta make blue, yellow and magenta make red, cyan and yellow make green, and all three make black. Adding black darkens the colour and taking away black lightens the colour.
